A BILL TO BE ENTITLED

AN ACT to revise the state plan for mental health, developmental disabilities, and substance abuse to ensure the state develops a comprehensive plan that provides a mission, A vision, goals, objectives, oversight, and accountability for the behavioral health care system in this state.

The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

 

part i. comprehensive plan for BEHAVIORAL health services

SECTION 1.(a)  The General Assembly finds that the Department of Health and Human Services (Department) has the responsibility to provide a framework, direction, oversight, and accountability for the State‑funded behavioral health care system. The General Assembly further finds that without clearly articulated goals as a basis for accountability, the State‑ and locally funded behavioral health care system cannot operate as a structured and unified system designed to meet the needs and expectations of the people of this State, and that a Comprehensive Plan is needed to ensure those needs and expectations are met.

SECTION 1.(b)  Article 4 of Chapter 122C of the General Statutes is amended by adding a new section to read:

"§ 122C‑102A.  Comprehensive Plan for Behavioral Health Services.

(a)        Purpose of State Comprehensive Plan. – The Department shall develop and implement a State Comprehensive Plan for Behavioral Health Services in this State. For purposes of the Comprehensive Plan, behavioral health services shall include mental health, developmental disabilities, substance abuse, and traumatic brain injury services. The purpose of the State Comprehensive Plan is to provide a framework to ensure the Department can oversee and monitor Medicaid, NC Health Choice, and other State‑funded behavioral health services, managed by LME/MCOs and health care providers. The Comprehensive Plan shall be submitted to the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Medicaid and NC Health Choice no later than January 31 of each year.

The Department shall ensure the Comprehensive Plan is implementable and shall facilitate cooperation and support from LME/MCOs, county programs, behavioral health service providers, and consumers in developing the mission, vision, goals, and objectives of the Comprehensive Plan. In order to increase the ability of the State, LME/MCOs, county programs, and behavioral health providers, to successfully implement the Comprehensive Plan, the Department shall not adopt or implement policies that are inconsistent with the Comprehensive Plan.

(b)        Content of the Comprehensive Plan. – The Comprehensive Plan shall include all of the following:

(1)        A mission, developed by the Department, that clearly states the purpose and priorities of Medicaid, NC Health Choice, and other State‑funded behavioral health services.

(2)        A 10‑year vision of the future, developed by the Department, as it pertains to the State behavioral health system and the individuals served by that system if the mission of the Comprehensive Plan is achieved.

(3)        Three‑year goals, developed by the Department in collaboration with LME/MCOs, that identify the areas where results will be achieved relative to the mission and vision of the Comprehensive Plan.

(4)        Specific, measurable 12‑month objectives that will be achieved to support the goals of the Comprehensive Plan.

(5)        Activities to be undertaken to attain the objectives outlined in the Comprehensive Plan. Each should identify at least the following specifics:

a.         The activity to be undertaken.

b.         The person or parties responsible for undertaking the activity.

c.         A time line for completing the activity.

d.         Identification of the end of the activity and indications for determining that the activity has been accomplished.

(c)        Assessment to Support Development of the Comprehensive Plan. – To support the development of the Comprehensive Plan, the Department shall complete an assessment of the adequacy of the current system, an identification of need or areas of improvement, proposed solutions, and progress updates for all of the following:

a.         Staffing levels, training, and competency in the Division of MH/DD/SA, the Division of Health Benefits, the LME/MCOs, and relevant behavioral health providers.

b.         Access to behavioral health services provided by the most appropriate provider measured by at least all of the following standards:

1.         Geographic location.

2.         Travel time.

3.         Wait time for appointments.

4.         Emergency department hold times.

c.         Solvency, financial management, and financial performance of the LME/MCOs, including strategic and operational plans, and reinvestment plans approved by the Department.

d.         Need and utilization of behavioral health services broken down by covered population and service type.

e.         Coordination and integration of behavioral health care services, including communication with, from, and between providers.

f.          Appropriateness of services and treatment effectiveness.

(d)       Comprehensive Plan Outcomes Data. – The Department shall define outcomes and data to measure progress on goals and objectives contained in the Comprehensive Plan for at least the following areas:

a.         Employment.

b.         Education.

c.         Homelessness.

d.         Imprisonment.

e.         Social determinants of health."

SECTION 2.  The Department of Health and Human Services shall identify a third‑party vendor to assist with the completion of the Comprehensive Plan, enacted in Section 1 of this act, in the event that the Department is unable to complete the Comprehensive Plan in a timely manner. If, by January 1 of the year in which the Comprehensive Plan is due, the Department identifies that the Comprehensive Plan (i) will not be submitted to the Joint Legislative Oversight on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Medicaid and NC Health Choice by the January 31 deadline or (ii) will not comply with the requirement of G.S. 122C‑10A, then the Department shall engage the services of the identified third‑party vendor to complete the Comprehensive Plan. The Department shall report to the Joint Legislative Oversight on Health and Human Services and the Joint Legislative Oversight Committee on Medicaid and NC Health Choice that the vendor will be completing the Comprehensive Plan and the anticipated deadline for completion. No additional funds shall be appropriated for this purpose.
