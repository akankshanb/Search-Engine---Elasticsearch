"§ 18C‑102.  Purpose and intent.

The General Assembly declares that the purpose of this Chapter is to establish a Lottery Commission to regulate and maintain a State‑operated lottery and other gaming activities to generate funds for the public purposes described in this Chapter.

"§ 18C‑103.  Definitions.

As used in this Chapter, unless the context requires otherwise:

(1)        "Commission" means the North Carolina State Lottery Commission.

(2)        "Commissioner" means a member of the Commission.

(3)        "Director" means the person selected by the Commission to be the chief administrator of the North Carolina State Lottery.

(4)        "Game" or "lottery game" means any procedure or amusement authorized by the Commission where prizes are distributed among persons who have paid, or unconditionally agreed to pay, for tickets or shares that provide the opportunity to win those prizes and does not utilize a video gaming machine as defined in G.S. 14‑306.1(c).

(4a)      "Licensed organization" means an organization licensed by the Commission to hold off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering pursuant to this Chapter and rules promulgated by the Commission.

(5)        "Lottery" means any lottery game or series of games established and operated pursuant to this Chapter.

(6)        "Lottery contractor" means a person other than a lottery retailer with whom the Commission has contracted for the purpose of providing goods or services to the Commission on an ongoing basis.

(6a)      "Lottery supplier" means a person, other than a lottery retailer, with whom the Commission has contracted for the purpose of providing goods or services to the Commission for an individual purchase which may include a maintenance program.

(6b)      "Pari‑mutuel wagering" means a form of wagering on the outcome of horse races in which wagers are purchased on a horse or horses and all wagers for each race are pooled and held by the licensed organization for distribution.

(6c)      "Pari‑mutuel pool" means the total money wagered by individuals on any horse or horses in a particular horse race to win, place, or show and held by the licensed organization for pari‑mutuel wagering.

(7)        "Person" means any natural person or corporation, limited liability company, trust, association, partnership, joint venture, subsidiary, or other business entity.

(7a)      "Potential contractor" or "lottery potential contractor" means any person other than a lottery retailer who submits a bid, proposal, or offer to procure a contract for goods or services for the Commission on an ongoing basis.

(8)        "Retailer", "lottery retailer", or "lottery game retailer" means a person with whom the Commission has contracted to sell tickets or shares in lottery games.

(9)        "Share" means any method of participation in a lottery game, other than by a ticket purchased on an equivalent basis with a ticket.

(10)      "Ticket" means any tangible evidence authorized by the Commission to demonstrate participation in a lottery game.

(11)      Repealed by Session Laws 2009‑357, s. 5, effective July 27, 2009.

"Article 9.

"Pari‑Mutuel Wagering.

"§ 18C‑180.  License required for holding pari‑mutuel wagering; exemptions.

(a)        No person shall hold off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering in this State without a license issued by the Commission and compliance with all provisions of this Article and all rules and regulations promulgated by the Commission.

(b)        Nothing in this Article shall be deemed to require licensure for any fair or other event sponsored by the State or any of its subdivisions.

"§ 18C‑181.  Licensed organizations; requirements.

(a)        Any person desiring to hold off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering in this State shall apply to the Commission for an organization license on a form to be prescribed by the Commission. The application shall contain at least all of the following:

(1)        The name and address of the entity applying for licensure.

(2)        A certified copy of any organizing documents for the entity applying for licensure.

(3)        To the extent applicable, the names and addresses of officers, directors, managers, shareholders, and partners of the entity applying for licensure.

(4)        The address of the locations where the off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering held by the entity applying for licensure will occur.

(5)        Any other information the Commission deems relevant.

(b)        No person shall own any silent or undisclosed interest in any entity applying for licensure. The Commission shall not issue a license to any applicant that fails to comply with the requirements of this section.

(c)        Off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering must be in compliance with 15 U.S.C. § 3001, et seq., and any other applicable laws. Wagers may be placed in separate pari‑mutuel pools or be combined with other licensed organizations located in this State.

(d)       It is unlawful for any person to conduct off‑track pari‑mutuel wagering without being currently licensed according to all necessary State and federal requirements.

(e)        The Commission shall charge an application fee for each application submitted in an amount not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000) for each location authorized in the application. Applications shall be renewed annually.

(f)        A licensed organization shall provide a place or establishment to its patrons at which the licensed organization has received Commission approval to conduct, operate, and supervise wagering and where the public may view the progress of wagering and the odds paid.

(g)        A licensed organization shall maintain records of all wagering activity for each off‑track race conducted, including dates, times, wagers placed, odds given, claims paid, breakage amounts, and any unclaimed payouts.

(h)        The Commission may deny, revoke, or suspend any license issued upon reason to believe a violation of this Article or any promulgated rule has occurred.

"§ 18C‑182.  Distribution of wager proceeds; inspection of records; audits; bond.

(a)        At least quarterly, an amount equaling twenty percent (20%) of all money wagered at each licensed location shall be remitted to the Commission by the licensed organization. The licensed organization shall also submit a copy of the wagering records recorded for the relevant period. Amounts remitted to the Commission pursuant to this subsection shall be credited to the North Carolina State Lottery Fund established in G.S. 18C‑160.

(b)        Winnings that remain unclaimed after the period set by the Commission for claiming the winnings shall not be considered abandoned property. If a valid claim is not made for winnings within the applicable period, the unclaimed winnings shall be handled in accordance with rules adopted by the Commission.

(c)        The Commission shall periodically inspect the wagering records maintained by a licensed organization. As a condition of licensure, the Commission may require an audit of wagering records by an independent certified public accountant and the posting of a bond in an amount not to exceed one million dollars ($1,000,000).

(d)       All winnings are subject to the State income tax.

"§ 18C‑183.  Persons prohibited from wagering.

(a)        It shall be unlawful for a person to allow a person under the age of 18 years to participate in pari‑mutuel wagering. No person under the age of 18 years shall participate in pari‑mutuel wagering. No winnings shall be paid to a person under the age of 18. A person who violates this subsection shall be guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor.

(b)        It shall be a defense for the person who operates pari‑mutuel wagering in violation of subsection (a) of this section if the person does either of the following:

(1)        Shows that the participant produced a valid drivers license, a special identification card issued under G.S. 20‑37.7, a military identification card, or a passport, showing the participant to be at least 18 years old and bearing a physical description of the person named on the card that reasonably describes the participant.

(2)        Produces evidence of other facts that reasonably indicated at the time of sale that the participant was at least 18 years old.

(c)        No wager shall be made by, and no winnings shall be paid to, a member of the Commission, the Director, or employee of the Commission, or to any spouse, parent, or child living in the same household as a person disqualified by this subsection.

"§ 18C‑184.  Rule making.

The Commission shall establish, by rule, all necessary regulations, forms, and procedures needed to carry out the provisions of this Article."

SECTION 2.  This act becomes effective January 1, 2020.
