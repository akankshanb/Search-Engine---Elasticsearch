A BILL TO BE ENTITLED

AN ACT to decrease the time required to obtain licensure by reciprocity awarded from the north carolina state board of barber examiners and to modify fees charged by the board.

The General Assembly of North Carolina enacts:

SECTION 1.  G.S. 86A‑12 reads as rewritten:

"§ 86A‑12.  Applicants licensed in other states.

(a)        The Board shall issue, without examination, a license to applicants already licensed in another state provided the applicant presents evidence satisfactory to the Board that:

(1)        He The applicant is currently an active, competent practitioner in good standing; andstanding.

(2)        He The applicant has practiced at least three one out of the five years immediately preceding his application; andapplication in this State.

(3)        He The applicant currently holds a valid license in another state; andstate.

(4)        There is no disciplinary proceeding or unresolved complaint pending against him the applicant at the time a license is to be issued by this State; andState.

(5)        The licensure requirements in the other state are the substantive equivalent of those required by this State.

(b)        The requirements in subdivisions (1) or (5), or both, of subsection (a) of this section may be waived by the Board provided that the applicant presents evidence satisfactory to the Board that the applicant:

(1)        Has met the licensure requirements of the state in which he the applicant received his license;the license.

(2)        Has at least five years of practical experience; andexperience.

(3)        Demonstrates his knowledge of barbering skills and of the sanitary regulations in North Carolina by passing a practical, written or oral examination.

(c)        Any license granted pursuant to this section is subject to the same duties and obligations and entitled to the same rights and privileges as a license issued under G.S. 86A‑3."
